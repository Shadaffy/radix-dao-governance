# Ethics Reporting Policy

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy provides a safe, structured channel for reporting governance-level misconduct and integrity violations by individuals holding authority within the DAO.

It protects reporters from retaliation, ensures reports are handled impartially, and provides accountability for those in positions of trust.

**Scope distinction:**

* The **Code of Conduct** covers behavioral violations (harassment, discrimination, bad faith conduct) by any DAO participant
* This policy covers **governance integrity violations** — specifically misuse of DAO authority by role-holders: RAC members, Working Group Stewards, Authorized Signers, and contracted persons acting in a decision-making capacity

Where both policies are relevant to a report, they are applied concurrently.

---

## 2. What Can Be Reported

Reports under this policy concern:

* **Abuse of role** — using a DAO position to advance personal interests, affiliated entities, or third parties at the expense of the DAO
* **Undisclosed conflicts of interest** — participating in decisions with an undisclosed material conflict (see **Conflict of Interest Policy**)
* **Misappropriation of funds** — directing DAO resources outside approved governance decisions
* **Governance manipulation** — actions designed to improperly influence governance outcomes, including coordinated vote manipulation, suppression of proposals, or interference with the RAC's oversight function
* **Breach of confidentiality** — misusing non-public DAO information for personal gain or competitive advantage
* **Failure to act** — deliberate neglect of a defined governance duty that causes material harm to the DAO
* **False reporting** — submitting materially false information to governance processes (proposals, applications, audits)

Reports about ordinary operational disagreements, policy disputes, or poor judgment (without intent) are better addressed through the **Dispute Resolution & Arbitration Policy**.

---

## 3. Who Can Report

Any person may submit a report under this policy, including:

* Token holders
* Current and former contributors and Stewards
* Contractors and vendors engaged by the DAO
* Third parties with relevant evidence of a violation

---

## 4. How to Report

### 4.1 Reporting Channels

Reports may be submitted to:

* **Standard reports** — the RAC via the designated private reporting channel published in the DAO's public documentation repository
* **Reports involving RAC members** — the Governance & Legal Working Group via its designated channel
* **Reports involving the entire RAC or a majority of RAC members** — directly to the DAO community via a governance forum post requesting an independent review panel (see Section 6.3)

The current reporting channel details are maintained in the DAO's public documentation repository.

---

### 4.2 Report Contents

A report should include:

* Description of the alleged violation and which policy provision it engages
* The role-holder(s) involved
* Date(s) and context of the conduct
* Supporting evidence (documents, transaction records, communications)
* Whether the reporter wishes to be kept informed of the outcome
* Whether the reporter requests confidentiality of their identity

Incomplete reports are accepted and will be triaged. The quality of evidence affects what action can be taken, but does not prevent a report from being received.

---

### 4.3 Anonymous Reports

Anonymous reports are accepted. The RAC or Governance & Legal WG will investigate to the extent possible without being able to contact the reporter. Anonymous reports may limit the ability to gather additional evidence or to fully substantiate findings.

---

## 5. Whistleblower Protections

### 5.1 Protection Against Retaliation

Any person who submits a report in good faith under this policy is protected from retaliation. Retaliation includes:

* Removal from a DAO role or engagement in response to a report
* Exclusion from governance processes, channels, or communications
* Harassment, intimidation, or hostile conduct directed at the reporter
* Negative characterization in DAO public records in response to a report

Retaliation against a reporter is itself a serious violation of the **Code of Conduct §4** and this policy, and will be treated as an independent matter for investigation.

---

### 5.2 Identity Protection

The reporter's identity is not disclosed to the subject of the report or to the public without the reporter's consent, except where:

* The reporter consents to disclosure
* Disclosure is required by applicable law
* The report is found to be made in bad faith (see Section 5.3)

Where identity protection cannot be maintained due to the nature of the evidence, the reporter will be informed before the investigation proceeds.

---

### 5.3 Bad Faith Reports

This policy does not protect reports made in bad faith — for example, fabricated allegations intended to harm a role-holder or manipulate governance. Where an investigation concludes that a report was made in bad faith, the reporter may be subject to **Code of Conduct** enforcement and the report will be publicly noted as unsubstantiated.

---

## 6. Investigation Process

### 6.1 Standard Process (Reports to RAC)

1. **Acknowledgement** — RAC acknowledges receipt within **3 business days**
2. **Initial triage** — RAC determines whether the report falls within scope of this policy within **5 business days** of acknowledgement. Out-of-scope reports are redirected to the appropriate channel
3. **Investigation** — RAC (excluding any member named in the report) conducts an investigation:
   * Reviews submitted evidence
   * May request additional information from the reporter
   * Gives the subject of the report a written summary of the allegations and an opportunity to respond
   * May consult the Governance & Legal WG for legal or procedural advice
4. **Finding** — RAC issues a written finding within **30 days** of acknowledgement:
   * Substantiated — the allegation is supported by evidence; recommended remedies are specified
   * Unsubstantiated — insufficient evidence to support the allegation
   * Inconclusive — investigation could not resolve the matter; further governance action may be needed
5. **Publication** — A summary of the finding (anonymised where appropriate) is published to the governance forum

---

### 6.2 Process Where a RAC Member Is Named

Where the report concerns one or more RAC members:

* The named member(s) are recused from all aspects of the investigation
* The remaining RAC members conduct the investigation
* If recusals leave fewer than two uninvolved RAC members, the matter is referred to the Governance & Legal WG to lead the investigation with RAC support
* Findings are published in full to the governance forum

---

### 6.3 Process Where the Majority of the RAC Is Named

Where a report implicates a majority of RAC members, or where confidence in the RAC's impartiality is otherwise compromised:

* The reporter or any token holder may post a governance forum request for an independent review
* The DAO community nominates and approves (via Signaling proposal) an independent review panel of three community members with no conflicts
* The panel conducts the investigation and publishes findings directly to the governance forum
* The panel may recommend governance proposals including RAC removal or restructuring

---

### 6.4 Interim Measures

Where the alleged conduct poses an ongoing risk to the DAO (e.g., a Signer continuing to have treasury access during a misappropriation investigation), the RAC may implement interim protective measures before the investigation concludes, including:

* Temporary suspension of the subject's access to DAO systems
* Placing a hold on relevant treasury transactions

Interim measures must be disclosed publicly within **48 hours** of being applied.

---

## 7. Outcomes and Remedies

Substantiated findings may result in one or more of the following, depending on severity:

* **Formal censure** — published finding of violation, recorded in the DAO's public records
* **Mandatory remediation** — required actions to correct harm (e.g., repayment of misappropriated funds via governance proposal)
* **Role suspension or removal** — per **Elections & Role Governance Policy §10**
* **Exclusion from future roles** — governance proposal to bar the individual from holding DAO authority roles for a defined period
* **Referral to legal authorities** — for conduct constituting potential criminal activity
* **Governance proposal to recover funds** — where misappropriation is substantiated

All outcomes are published to the governance forum as part of the finding summary.

---

## 8. Appeals

A subject found to have committed a violation may appeal the finding:

* Appeal must be submitted to the RAC (or Governance & Legal WG if the RAC led the investigation) within **14 days** of the finding
* The appeal is reviewed by a panel that excludes anyone involved in the original investigation
* If no impartial panel can be formed internally, the appeal is submitted as a governance proposal for community review
* The appeal panel may uphold, modify, or overturn the original finding

---

## 9. Record-Keeping

The RAC maintains a confidential log of all reports received under this policy, including:

* Date received
* Nature of allegation (category, not identifying details)
* Outcome
* Any remedies applied

An anonymised aggregate summary (number of reports by category and outcome) is included in the RAC's Quarterly Report per the **Transparency and Reporting Policy §7.2**.

Individual report details remain confidential except where a finding is published.

---

## 10. Relationship to Other Policies

* **Code of Conduct §5** — Reporting channel for behavioral violations by any participant; retaliation against reporters is a Code of Conduct violation
* **Conflict of Interest Policy §10** — Enforcement pathway for undisclosed conflict violations
* **Dispute Resolution & Arbitration Policy §9** — Complementary misconduct handling; this policy provides the structured whistleblower channel that §9 references
* **Elections & Role Governance Policy §10** — Role removal procedures triggered by substantiated findings
* **Transparency and Reporting Policy §7.2** — Anonymised reporting of ethics case outcomes in RAC quarterly reports

---

## 11. Amendments

This policy may be updated via a Governance Process proposal.

Amendment procedures and classification requirements are defined in the **Governance Maintenance & Upgrade Framework**.
