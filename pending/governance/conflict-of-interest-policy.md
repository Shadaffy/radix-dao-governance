# Conflict of Interest Policy

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy ensures that DAO governance decisions are made in the best interests of the DAO and its community, free from undisclosed personal or financial interests.

It defines:

* What constitutes a conflict of interest
* Who must disclose and when
* How disclosures are made and recorded
* When recusal is required
* How violations are handled

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all individuals exercising decision-making authority within the DAO, including:

* RAC members
* Authorized Signers
* Working Group Stewards and members with budget or execution authority
* Strategic Coordination WG members
* Any contractor or advisor acting in a decision-making capacity on behalf of the DAO

Token holders exercising ordinary voting rights are not subject to mandatory disclosure under this policy, but are encouraged to act in the DAO's best interest.

---

## 3. What Constitutes a Conflict of Interest

A conflict of interest exists when a covered person's personal, financial, or professional interests could improperly influence — or reasonably appear to influence — their exercise of DAO authority.

### 3.1 Financial Interests

A conflict exists where a covered person, or a close associate (family member, business partner, employer), has a material financial interest in:

* An entity seeking DAO funding, a grant, or a contract
* A protocol, product, or project that would directly benefit from a DAO decision
* A competitor of an entity the DAO is evaluating for partnership or investment

**Material financial interest** means equity, tokens, debt, employment, or advisory compensation above a *de minimis* threshold as defined in DAO Parameters.

---

### 3.2 Organizational Affiliations

A conflict exists where a covered person holds a paid role, directorship, or advisory position with:

* An entity submitting a proposal or grant application to the DAO
* Any entity — including predecessor organisations, protocol developers, founding sponsors, or their successors and asset transferees — that has or had a material controlling, funding, or developmental relationship with the Radix protocol or DAO infrastructure
* Any foundation, company, or protocol with a direct stake in DAO governance outcomes

---

### 3.3 Personal Relationships

A conflict may exist where a close personal relationship (family, romantic partner, close friend) with a proposal submitter or counterparty could influence the covered person's judgment.

---

### 3.4 Simultaneous Role Conflicts

A conflict may exist where a covered person holds multiple DAO roles whose interests or authorities may be in tension — for example, a Working Group Steward who is also a grant applicant to that same Working Group.

---

## 4. Disclosure Requirements

### 4.1 Initial Disclosure

All covered persons must submit a Conflicts Disclosure Statement upon:

* Nomination or appointment to any covered role
* Commencement of a contract or advisory engagement with the DAO

The disclosure must include:

* All current financial interests in ecosystem entities (above *de minimis* threshold)
* All current organizational affiliations, including paid roles, directorships, and advisory positions
* Any personal relationships that could constitute a conflict
* Any other circumstances the covered person believes could give rise to a perceived conflict

Initial disclosures are reviewed by the RAC and published in the DAO's public records.

---

### 4.2 Ongoing Disclosure

Covered persons must update their disclosure within **14 days** of any material change, including:

* Accepting a new paid role or advisory position with an ecosystem entity
* Acquiring a material financial interest in an entity relevant to DAO decisions
* The emergence of a new personal relationship relevant to this policy

---

### 4.3 Transaction-Level Disclosure

Before participating in any DAO decision, covered persons must declare any conflict with the specific matter under discussion, regardless of whether it was previously disclosed in their standing disclosure.

Transaction-level declarations must be made:

* In the relevant governance forum thread or Working Group meeting record
* Before deliberation or voting takes place
* In sufficient detail for other participants to assess the nature of the conflict

---

## 5. Recusal

### 5.1 When Recusal is Required

A covered person must recuse themselves from any decision where they have a material conflict of interest, including:

* Abstaining from vote or sign-off on the conflicted matter
* Withdrawing from deliberations and discussions where their participation could improperly influence the outcome
* Not acting as a representative of the DAO in negotiations or communications with the conflicted party

---

### 5.2 Self-Recusal

Covered persons are responsible for identifying and self-declaring recusal obligations. When in doubt, they should err on the side of recusal and seek guidance from the RAC.

---

### 5.3 Requested Recusal

Any other covered person or the RAC may request recusal of a covered person for a specific matter. The request must state the grounds.

If the covered person disputes the recusal request, the RAC makes a binding determination within **5 business days**.

---

### 5.4 Recusal Record

All recusals must be recorded in the meeting minutes or governance forum thread for the relevant decision. The record must state:

* The person recusing
* The nature of the conflict
* The decision from which they recused

---

## 6. Prohibited Conduct

The following are prohibited regardless of disclosure:

* **Self-dealing** — approving DAO resources for a transaction in which the covered person has a direct financial interest, even if disclosed, unless explicitly approved by the DAO through an arms-length governance process
* **Undisclosed conflicts** — participating in decisions where a material conflict exists without prior disclosure
* **Misuse of position** — using a DAO role to steer decisions, information, or resources toward personal or affiliated interests
* **Trading on non-public information** — using non-public DAO information to make financial decisions in connected markets or tokens

---

## 7. Legacy Entity and Commercial Entity Relationships

The following provisions apply to any covered person with prior or residual affiliations to predecessor entities or their successors:

* Any covered person who holds, or has held within the preceding three years, equity, a paid role, or non-trivial token compensation from any predecessor entity, successor, or asset transferee with a material relationship to the Radix protocol or DAO infrastructure, must disclose this as a standing conflict
* Such persons must recuse from any DAO decision that would provide material financial benefit to those entities, their successors, or their former principals, relative to other ecosystem participants
* This does not prevent persons with prior affiliations from holding DAO roles, but any residual conflicts must be publicly on record

This provision is intended to ensure transparency during and after the transition from predecessor commercial entities to community governance — not to exclude individuals with relevant expertise.

---

## 8. RAC Obligations

RAC members are subject to heightened obligations given their oversight role:

* A RAC member with a conflict on any matter the RAC is adjudicating must recuse from that specific determination
* If a conflict affects a majority of RAC members on a given matter, it must be escalated to a community governance vote per the **Dispute Resolution & Arbitration Policy**
* RAC members must not hold positions at entities currently under RAC review

---

## 9. Record-Keeping

The RAC maintains a **Conflicts Register** containing:

* All current standing disclosures from covered persons
* All transaction-level conflict declarations
* All recusal records

The Conflicts Register is published and updated no less than quarterly. Each covered person's standing disclosure must be accessible to the community at all times.

---

## 10. Enforcement

Suspected violations of this policy are handled as follows:

1. A report may be submitted to the RAC (or, if the violation involves a RAC member, to the Governance & Legal WG)
2. The RAC investigates and issues a finding within **30 days**
3. Where a violation is confirmed, available remedies include:
   * Invalidation of decisions made in violation of this policy
   * Removal from role (per **Elections & Role Governance Policy** §10)
   * Referral to the **Code of Conduct** enforcement process for sanctions
   * In cases of fraud or self-dealing, a governance proposal to recover misappropriated funds

The accused person is given the opportunity to respond before a finding is issued.

---

## 11. Relationship to Other Policies

* **Code of Conduct** — Disclosure failures and self-dealing are also Code of Conduct violations
* **Elections & Role Governance Policy §5, §15** — Eligibility requirements and conflict disclosures at nomination
* **Authorized Signers Rules** — Conflict obligations for signers acting on treasury transactions
* **Dispute Resolution & Arbitration Policy** — Escalation path for contested recusal determinations
