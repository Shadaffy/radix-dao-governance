# Transparency and Reporting Policy

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the DAO's standards for public disclosure, financial reporting, and accountability communication.

Transparency is a Core Principle of the DAO Charter. This policy operationalises it by defining:

* What must be reported and by whom
* How frequently reports must be published
* The format and minimum content standards for each report type
* Where records are published and stored
* How the community can verify compliance

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* The RAC
* All Working Groups
* Authorized Signers (treasury reporting)
* The DAO as a whole (aggregate financial reporting)

It does not govern confidentiality exceptions, which are defined in the **Charter §3.2** and the **Execution & Treasury Actions Policy §10**.

---

## 3. Guiding Principles

### 3.1 Default to Public

All governance actions, decisions, financial flows, and operational outcomes are public by default. Confidentiality is a narrow exception requiring specific justification and retrospective disclosure.

---

### 3.2 Timely Disclosure

Reports must be published on the schedule defined in this policy. Late or missing reports are a breach of mandate and must be flagged by the RAC.

---

### 3.3 Accessible Language

Reports must be written to be understandable by an informed community member, not only by insiders. Technical or financial jargon must be explained where used.

---

### 3.4 Immutable Record

Wherever possible, reports and governance records should be stored in a manner that is tamper-evident — on-chain, in a versioned public repository, or via a content-addressed system.

---

## 4. Governance Records (Ongoing)

The following must be published in near-real-time as they occur:

| Record | Publisher | Where |
|---|---|---|
| All submitted governance proposals | Governance interface | On-chain / forum |
| Voting outcomes (yes/no/abstain by wallet) | Governance interface | On-chain |
| Veto challenge filings and RAC determinations | RAC | Governance forum |
| RAC emergency actions | RAC | Governance forum |
| Conflict of interest disclosures (Conflicts Register) | RAC | Public DAO repository |
| Authorized Signer identities | RAC | Public DAO repository |
| Working Group contributor registries | Each WG | WG public channel |

These records form the permanent, auditable history of the DAO and must not be deleted or altered.

---

## 5. Working Group Reporting

### 5.1 Monthly Progress Updates

Each Working Group must publish a brief update once per calendar month covering:

* Work completed since the last update
* Work in progress and current status (On Track / At Risk / Off Track)
* Any blockers or risks
* Any budget variances (per **Working Group Framework §7.1**)

Format: short narrative or structured bullet points. Published to the WG's designated public channel.

> **Note:** Monthly updates replace the previous biweekly cadence. The quarterly report (§5.2) remains the primary accountability document; monthly updates serve as lightweight progress signals.

---

### 5.2 Quarterly Reports

Each Working Group must publish a Quarterly Report within **14 days** of the end of each quarter covering:

* Summary of outcomes delivered against objectives
* Budget summary: approved budget, actual spend, variance explanation
* Contributor compensation paid in the period (anonymised if legally required, otherwise named)
* Grants or RFPs issued and their status
* Key risks and issues
* Objectives for the next quarter
* Self-assessment against success metrics defined in the WG charter

Quarterly Reports are published to the governance forum and linked from the DAO's public documentation repository.

---

### 5.3 End-of-Term Retrospective

At the conclusion of each 6-month term, each Working Group must publish an End-of-Term Retrospective within **21 days** of term end covering:

* Full summary of outcomes against the original mandate
* Complete budget reconciliation
* Lessons learned and process improvements
* Handover notes for the incoming Stewards
* Recommendation on whether the WG should be renewed, restructured, or wound down

The Retrospective is presented to the DAO community and informs the renewal governance proposal.

---

## 6. Treasury and Financial Reporting

### 6.1 Monthly Treasury Snapshot

The Authorized Signers, in coordination with the RAC, must publish a Monthly Treasury Snapshot within **7 days** of each month end covering:

* Total treasury value by asset, in both native token and USD equivalent
* Wallet/account breakdown (operations, grants, reserves, etc.)
* Inflows during the month (with source)
* Outflows during the month (with recipient category and amount)
* Any pending approved-but-unexecuted transactions

---

### 6.2 Quarterly Financial Report

The RAC must publish a Quarterly Financial Report within **21 days** of each quarter end covering:

* Opening and closing treasury balance
* Full itemized income and expenditure for the quarter
* Budget vs. actuals across all approved Working Group budgets
* Grant disbursements by recipient and milestone
* Any unbudgeted expenditure and its authorization
* Forward-looking budget commitments for the next quarter

The Quarterly Financial Report is the primary accountability document for treasury stewardship. It must be published to the governance forum and archived in the DAO's public documentation repository.

---

### 6.3 Annual Financial Summary

The RAC must publish an Annual Financial Summary within **45 days** of the DAO's financial year end covering:

* Year-end treasury balance and asset composition
* Total income and expenditure for the year
* Summary of grants awarded and completed
* Summary of significant governance decisions with financial impact
* Assessment of treasury health and sustainability
* Any material risks to financial continuity

The Annual Financial Summary is a community-facing document and should be written to be accessible to all token holders.

---

## 7. RAC Reporting

### 7.1 Biweekly Activity Log

The RAC must publish a brief biweekly log covering:

* Proposals reviewed and any formal determinations made
* Veto challenges received and resolved
* Execution actions verified
* Any governance compliance issues flagged

---

### 7.2 Quarterly RAC Report

The RAC must publish a Quarterly Report within **21 days** of each quarter end covering:

* Summary of governance activity: proposals passed, failed, vetoed
* Execution compliance: any deviations from approved proposals
* Disputes and misconduct: anonymised summary of cases handled
* Conflicts Register updates
* Any systemic governance risks identified
* Status of all active emergency actions

---

## 8. Public Documentation Repository

The DAO must maintain a publicly accessible, versioned documentation repository containing:

* All current governance documents (Charter, policies, frameworks)
* The DAO Parameters Registry (current version)
* All Working Group charters (current and archived)
* The Conflicts Register
* All Quarterly and Annual reports
* The Governance Document Changelog

The repository must be maintained such that:

* Current versions are clearly distinguished from superseded versions
* The full version history is accessible
* The repository is accessible without login or registration

The Governance & Legal Working Group is responsible for the maintenance and integrity of the public documentation repository.

---

## 9. Reporting Failures

Where a reporting obligation is not met by the defined deadline:

1. The RAC flags the failure publicly within **5 business days**
2. The responsible body has **7 days** to publish the overdue report or submit a justified extension request
3. Persistent failure (two consecutive missed deadlines of the **same report type** without justified extension) constitutes a breach of mandate and may trigger a removal review under the **Elections & Role Governance Policy §10**

**Cure period:** If an overdue report is published within **14 days** of the original deadline (whether or not a justified extension was filed), the two-strike clock is reset. The cure period exists to allow for genuine delays without immediately triggering removal proceedings; it is not a de facto extension of the reporting deadline.

**Justified extension:** A justified extension may be requested in cases of personal illness, bereavement, technical outage, or other circumstances materially impairing the role-holder's ability to report. Extensions must be requested before the original deadline where possible, must specify a revised deadline (maximum 14 additional days from the original), and are granted at RAC discretion with a simple written acknowledgement. The RAC must respond to extension requests within 3 business days.

**Same-type rule:** The two-strike rule applies to consecutive misses of the same report type (e.g., two quarterly reports in a row, or two monthly updates in a row). Missing one monthly update and one quarterly report in the same period does not alone trigger a breach; a pattern within the same report type is required.

The RAC itself is not exempt. Where the RAC fails its own reporting obligations, any token holder may raise this via the governance forum, and the Governance & Legal WG must investigate.

---

## 10. Confidentiality Exceptions

Reporting obligations may be partially fulfilled on a confidential basis only where:

* Full disclosure would create a material security risk (e.g., disclosing active security incident details before remediation)
* Full disclosure would breach a legal obligation (e.g., ongoing legal proceedings)
* The RAC has approved a specific, time-limited confidentiality exception in writing

In all cases, the existence of the confidentiality exception must itself be publicly disclosed. The underlying information must be disclosed retrospectively once the grounds for confidentiality no longer apply.
