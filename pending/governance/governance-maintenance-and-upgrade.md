# Governance Maintenance & Upgrade Framework

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how governance documents are:

* maintained
* updated
* evolved over time

---

## 2. Guiding Principle

> Governance must be adaptable without compromising integrity.

---

## 3. Document Hierarchy

Governance documents follow this hierarchy:

1. Charter (highest authority)
2. Governance Policies
3. DAO Parameters Registry

If conflicts arise between governance documents:

* higher-level documents prevail

---

### 3.1 Legal Formation Documents

The Formation Documents (Operating Agreement, Certificate of Formation, BOIR Template, Asset Transfer Agreement, IP Schedule, and Continuity Statement) are legal instruments filed with the Registered Agent. They are not governance policies within the hierarchy above and are not subordinate to governance policy documents.

Conflict resolution between layers:

* Where the Operating Agreement expressly delegates a matter to governance policy (e.g., "as defined in the operational policies adopted under §12.4"), the governance policy controls that matter.
* Where the Operating Agreement and a governance policy conflict on a matter the Operating Agreement does not delegate, the Operating Agreement prevails.
* The Charter prevails over both. No governance policy or formation document may contradict the Charter.
* The Legal Wrapper complements the Charter and governance documents; it does not override governance authority but enables real-world execution of DAO decisions. Where the Legal Wrapper and a governance policy conflict, the RAC shall flag the conflict for governance resolution.

Formation Documents may be amended only by the process specified in the Operating Agreement (Article X):

* Changes to Articles I–IV or XI require a **Constitutional proposal** (≥66% YES, ≥10% quorum)
* Changes to Articles V–XII require a **Governance Process proposal** (≥60% YES, ≥7% quorum)

---

## 4. Types of Changes

---

### 4.1 Patch Changes

* cosmetic or non-semantic
* do not affect meaning (e.g., typo corrections, formatting, cross-reference updates)

Approval:

* **Governance Process proposal** — standard threshold (≥60% YES, ≥7% quorum)

---

### 4.2 Minor Changes

* process improvements
* parameter adjustments
* non-structural updates to policy language

Approval:

* **Governance Process proposal** — standard threshold (≥60% YES, ≥7% quorum)

---

### 4.3 Major Changes

* changes to the Charter
* changes to governance structure
* changes affecting authority, rights, or the role of any governance body

Approval:

* **Constitutional proposal** — supermajority threshold (≥66% YES, ≥10% quorum)

All thresholds are defined in DAO Parameters §3.2 and §3.3.

---

## 5. Amendment Process

All changes must:

* be proposed through governance
* include clear rationale
* specify change type (patch / minor / major)
* include version updates

---

## 6. Versioning

Each update must include:

* version number
* summary of changes
* effective date

---

## 7. Consistency Rules

Changes must:

* not conflict with higher-level documents
* maintain internal consistency across policies
* clearly state any dependencies

---

## 8. Maintenance Responsibility

* Governance & Legal Support WG maintains documents
* RAC ensures process compliance
* DAO approves all substantive changes

---

## 9. Emergency Amendments

In urgent cases:

* Emergency Policy may apply
* temporary measures allowed
* must be ratified by DAO

---

## 10. Transparency

All changes must be:

* publicly visible
* clearly documented
* traceable over time

---

## 11. Guiding Principle

> Governance evolves through **clear rules**, not ad-hoc decisions.
