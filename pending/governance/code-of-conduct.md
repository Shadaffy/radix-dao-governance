# Code of Conduct

---

## 1. Scope

This document defines the behavioral standards expected of all participants in the Radix DAO, including:

* Token holders participating in governance
* Working Group members and contributors
* Elected and appointed role-holders (RAC members, Authorized Signers, WG Leads)
* Contractors and vendors acting on behalf of the DAO
* Any person participating in DAO-affiliated forums, channels, or events

---

## 2. Purpose

The DAO's ability to govern effectively depends on the trust, integrity, and good faith of its participants. This Code of Conduct:

* Establishes the minimum standards of behavior expected in the DAO
* Protects the safety and dignity of all participants
* Provides a clear and consistent basis for addressing misconduct
* Supports the DAO's Core Principles of Transparency and Accountability as defined in the Charter

---

## 3. Core Standards

### 3.1 Good Faith Participation

All participants must:

* Act honestly and in good faith in all governance activities
* Represent their intentions and affiliations accurately
* Not misuse governance processes for personal gain at the expense of the DAO or community
* Not attempt to manipulate, coerce, or deceive other participants

---

### 3.2 Respectful Conduct

All participants must:

* Treat others with dignity and respect regardless of background, identity, or opinion
* Engage in disagreement through reasoned argument, not personal attacks
* Not engage in harassment, intimidation, or threatening behavior
* Not engage in hate speech, discrimination, or derogatory language targeting individuals or groups

---

### 3.3 Transparency and Disclosure

All participants must:

* Disclose relevant affiliations, financial interests, or conflicts when participating in governance decisions (see also **Conflict of Interest Policy**)
* Accept the applicable KYC/AML compliance process before receiving any grant, compensation, or other disbursement from the DAO (see **Contributor Compensation Policy §3.5** and **Grant Program Policy §4**)
* Not impersonate other participants or misrepresent their identity or role
* Not act under multiple identities (Sybil behavior) to artificially amplify influence

---

### 3.4 Protection of DAO Resources

All participants must:

* Not misappropriate, waste, or redirect DAO resources for unauthorized purposes
* Not exploit governance processes or smart contract vulnerabilities to extract unauthorized value
* Report known vulnerabilities responsibly (see **Security Disclosure & Bug Bounty Policy**)

---

### 3.5 Confidentiality

Where participants are granted access to non-public DAO information (e.g., pre-disclosure of security issues, legal matters, pending proposals under embargo):

* Such information must not be shared outside authorized channels
* Such information must not be used for personal financial gain (e.g., trading on non-public information)

---

## 4. Prohibited Conduct

The following constitute serious violations:

* **Harassment** — repeated unwanted contact, intimidation, or abuse targeting any participant
* **Discrimination** — treating participants differently based on protected characteristics including but not limited to race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religion, disability, or nationality
* **Bribery or vote-buying** — offering or accepting compensation in exchange for governance votes
* **Collusion** — coordinating with others to manipulate governance outcomes in ways that harm the DAO or circumvent its processes
* **Sybil attacks** — using multiple wallets or identities to gain disproportionate influence
* **Fraud** — submitting false information in proposals, grant applications, or role applications
* **Doxxing** — publicly revealing private information about participants without consent
* **Retaliation** — taking adverse action against participants who report misconduct or raise governance concerns in good faith

---

## 5. Reporting Misconduct

### 5.1 How to Report

Misconduct may be reported to:

* The RAC via the designated reporting channel
* Any Working Group Lead (for WG-internal conduct issues)
* The Governance & Legal Working Group for serious violations

Reports should include:

* Description of the conduct
* Date(s) and context
* Evidence where available
* Identity of the reporting party (reports may be made confidentially where circumstances require)

---

### 5.2 Confidentiality of Reports

* The identity of reporters will be protected where disclosure would place the reporter at risk
* Reporters acting in good faith are protected against retaliation (see Section 4)
* Anonymous reports will be considered but may limit the DAO's ability to investigate or act

---

### 5.3 Good Faith Reporting

This policy does not protect reports made in bad faith to harm another participant. Deliberate false reports constitute a violation of this Code.

---

## 6. Enforcement

### 6.1 Responsible Body

The RAC is responsible for reviewing reported violations involving role-holders, elected members, and Working Group participants.

The Governance & Legal Working Group may assist with investigation where instructed by the RAC.

---

### 6.2 Review Process

Upon receiving a report:

1. The RAC acknowledges receipt within **5 business days**
2. The RAC conducts a proportionate investigation
3. The accused participant is given an opportunity to respond before a determination is made
4. The RAC issues a finding and recommended remedy within **30 days** of acknowledgement
5. Serious violations (bribery, fraud, harassment) may result in immediate suspension of DAO access pending investigation

---

### 6.3 Sanctions

Sanctions are proportionate to the severity and circumstances of the violation. Available remedies include:

* **Warning** — formal written notice
* **Temporary suspension** — restricted participation in governance for a defined period
* **Removal from role** — for role-holders, removal from elected or appointed position
* **Permanent exclusion** — barred from participation in DAO-affiliated spaces and processes
* **Referral to legal authorities** — for conduct that may constitute criminal behavior
* **Clawback recommendation** — for grant or compensation recipients found to have committed fraud, a governance proposal to recover funds

---

### 6.5 Sanctions Enforceability and KYC Identity Binding

The practical enforceability of sanctions depends on whether the sanctioned participant has completed KYC verification:

* **KYC-verified participants** (compensated contributors, grant recipients, UBO token holders): Sanctions are bound to the participant's verified identity and are not limited to specific wallet addresses or pseudonyms. A sanctioned individual may not circumvent a suspension or exclusion by operating under a different wallet or account. Any future KYC verification submitted by a sanctioned individual will be cross-checked against the sanctions record.

* **Non-KYC participants** (ordinary token holders operating pseudonymously): Sanctions are wallet- and account-based. The DAO acknowledges this limits enforceability. Where serious sanctions are imposed on a pseudonymous participant, the RAC may require KYC verification as a condition of any future grant application, compensated engagement, or formal governance role, effectively making KYC the gateway to continued participation beyond ordinary token voting.

The RAC maintains a sanctions record alongside the KYC register. KYC providers engaged by the DAO must be instructed to flag applicants appearing on the sanctions record.

---

### 6.4 Appeal

Any participant subject to a sanction may appeal the determination by:

* Submitting a written appeal to the RAC within **14 days** of the finding
* Requesting that the appeal be reviewed by a panel that excludes any RAC members involved in the original finding

If the RAC is unable to form an impartial panel, the appeal may be escalated to a community governance vote per the **Dispute Resolution & Arbitration Policy**.

---

## 7. Scope of Spaces

This Code applies to conduct in:

* DAO governance forums and interfaces
* Working Group meetings and communications
* DAO-affiliated social channels (Discord, Telegram, etc.)
* Public events and calls organized under DAO auspices
* On-chain governance participation

---

## 8. Relationship to Other Policies

This Code operates alongside:

* **Conflict of Interest Policy** — for disclosure and recusal obligations
* **Dispute Resolution & Arbitration Policy** — for escalated conflicts between participants
* **Ethics Reporting Policy** — for reporting governance-level misconduct by role-holders
* **Working Group Charters** — which may define additional conduct standards for WG members

In the event of conflict between this Code and another policy, the more specific policy applies within its defined scope.
