# Grant Program Policy

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how the DAO awards grants to external individuals, teams, and projects building on or contributing to the Radix ecosystem.

It establishes:

* Grant categories and eligibility
* The application and evaluation process
* Approval authority by grant size
* Milestone-based disbursement mechanics
* Reporting obligations and accountability
* Clawback and suspension provisions

This policy governs ecosystem grants to external parties. Compensation for DAO contributors and Working Group members is defined in the **Contributor Compensation Policy**.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to any funding request from an external party seeking DAO resources to:

* Build applications, tools, or infrastructure on the Radix network
* Conduct research benefiting the Radix ecosystem
* Create educational content or community resources
* Fund events, initiatives, or programs that advance the DAO's objectives

---

## 3. Grant Categories

### 3.1 Micro Grants

* Small, fast-turn engagements with clear short-term deliverables
* Threshold and approval authority defined in DAO Parameters
* Administered by the relevant Working Group within approved budget
* No governance vote required; Steward approval sufficient

---

### 3.2 Standard Grants

* Medium-scale projects with defined milestones over a defined timeframe
* Above the Micro Grant threshold and up to the Standard Grant ceiling (defined in DAO Parameters)
* Require governance vote via a Treasury & Budget Proposal
* Administered by the relevant Working Group

---

### 3.3 Strategic Grants

* Significant or long-term funding for high-impact ecosystem initiatives
* Above the Standard Grant ceiling
* Require governance vote via a Treasury & Budget Proposal with extended review period
* May include token-denominated components with vesting requirements

---

### 3.4 Emergency Ecosystem Grants

* Rapid response funding for critical ecosystem needs (e.g., security response, infrastructure continuity)
* Approved via Emergency Procedures defined in the **Emergency & Safeguards Policy**
* Subject to retrospective governance ratification within the timeframe defined in DAO Parameters

---

## 4. Eligibility

Any individual, team, or legal entity may apply for a grant, subject to:

* Not being under active investigation for fraud, misconduct, or misuse of prior DAO funds
* Not being subject to applicable international sanctions
* Having no unresolved obligations from a prior grant (outstanding milestones or reports due)
* Disclosure of any conflicts of interest per the **Conflict of Interest Policy**
* Having completed KYC verification through the DAO's designated process prior to any disbursement (Standard and Strategic grants); or having provided sufficient identity information for Micro Grants as determined by the administering Working Group

The DAO does not require applicants to be incorporated, but larger grants may require a legal entity for disbursement purposes.

---

## 5. Application Process

### 5.1 Expression of Interest (Optional)

Applicants are encouraged to post an Expression of Interest on the governance forum before submitting a formal application. This allows early community feedback and reduces wasted effort on ineligible or misaligned proposals.

---

### 5.2 Formal Application

A formal grant application must be submitted via the designated channel and include:

* **Project description** — what is being built or accomplished and why it benefits the Radix ecosystem
* **Team background** — relevant experience and prior work
* **Detailed budget** — itemized breakdown of requested funds and their intended use
* **Milestones** — defined deliverables with acceptance criteria and target dates
* **Timeline** — overall project duration
* **Success metrics** — how outcomes will be measured
* **Conflict disclosures** — any financial or organizational relationships relevant to the application
* **Prior funding** — disclosure of any other grants or funding received for the same or related work

---

### 5.3 Review Period

All formal applications have a minimum **14-day** community review period on the governance forum before evaluation begins. During this period:

* Community members may comment, ask questions, and raise concerns
* Applicants are expected to respond to substantive questions
* The relevant Working Group may request additional information

---

## 6. Evaluation

### 6.1 Evaluating Body

| Grant Type | Evaluating Body |
|---|---|
| Micro Grant | Relevant Working Group Stewards |
| Standard Grant | Relevant Working Group + RAC review |
| Strategic Grant | Governance vote with Working Group recommendation |

---

### 6.2 Evaluation Criteria

All applications are assessed against:

* **Alignment** — does the project advance the DAO's objectives and the Radix ecosystem?
* **Feasibility** — is the team capable of delivering the proposed work?
* **Value for funds** — is the budget proportionate to the expected outcomes?
* **Milestone quality** — are deliverables specific, measurable, and verifiable?
* **Risk** — what are the risks of non-delivery and how are they mitigated?
* **Duplication** — is similar work already funded or underway?

---

### 6.3 Evaluation Record

The evaluating body must publish a written evaluation record stating the outcome and the primary reasons for approval or rejection. This record is published on the governance forum.

---

## 7. Approval

### 7.1 Micro Grants

Approved by WG Stewards within delegated budget authority. Published in the WG's regular reporting.

---

### 7.2 Standard and Strategic Grants

Approved via DAO governance vote per the **Proposal & Voting Framework**.

The governance proposal must include:

* The approved application or a summary
* The evaluation record
* Proposed milestone schedule
* Total amount requested
* Administering Working Group

---

## 8. Grant Agreement

All approved grants (except Micro Grants below a threshold in DAO Parameters) require a signed Grant Agreement before disbursement begins.

The Grant Agreement must include:

* Parties and grant amount
* Approved milestones, deliverables, and acceptance criteria
* Payment schedule tied to milestone completion
* Reporting obligations
* IP and licensing terms (aligned with the **Open Source & Intellectual Property Policy**)
* Clawback provisions
* Jurisdiction and governing law

A standard Grant Agreement Template is maintained by the Governance & Legal Working Group.

---

## 9. Disbursement

### 9.1 Milestone-Based Disbursement

Grant funds are disbursed in tranches upon completion of agreed milestones:

* An initial tranche (up to 30% of total grant) may be disbursed at grant start to cover set-up costs
* Remaining tranches are released upon Steward sign-off that milestone deliverables have been met
* Disputed milestone completions are escalated to the RAC for determination

---

### 9.2 Lump-Sum Disbursement

Lump-sum disbursement in full at grant start is only permitted for:

* Micro Grants below the threshold in DAO Parameters
* Cases where the nature of the work makes milestone disbursement impractical, with specific governance approval

---

## 10. Reporting Obligations

Grant recipients must:

* Submit milestone reports to the administering Working Group upon completing each milestone
* Submit a final completion report within **30 days** of the grant end date
* Respond to reasonable requests for information from the Working Group or RAC within **14 days**

Reports must be published publicly unless the RAC approves an exception on security or legal grounds.

---

## 11. Grant Suspension and Clawback

### 11.1 Suspension

A grant may be suspended (disbursements paused) where:

* A milestone deadline is missed without agreed extension
* The recipient is unresponsive for more than **30 days**
* Evidence of misuse of funds or misrepresentation emerges
* The recipient is found to be in breach of the **Code of Conduct**

Suspension is triggered by the administering Working Group and notified to the RAC.

---

### 11.2 Termination and Clawback

A grant may be terminated and disbursed funds subject to clawback via a governance proposal where:

* Fraud or material misrepresentation is established
* Funds have been materially misused
* The grant recipient is in persistent breach after suspension

Clawback actions require a governance vote. Partial recovery is acceptable where full recovery is not possible.

---

### 11.3 Voluntary Return

Recipients who are unable to complete deliverables are encouraged to voluntarily return unused funds and notify the Working Group promptly.

---

## 12. Reapplication

A grant recipient who has failed to complete a prior grant may not reapply until:

* All outstanding reports and obligations from prior grants are resolved, and
* A cooling-off period of **6 months** from the termination date has elapsed (waivable by governance vote)

---

## 13. Conflicts of Interest

Working Group Stewards with a conflict of interest in a grant application must recuse from evaluation and approval per the **Conflict of Interest Policy**.

Grant applicants affiliated with Working Group Stewards or RAC members are not automatically ineligible, but such affiliations must be fully disclosed and the conflicted party must recuse.
