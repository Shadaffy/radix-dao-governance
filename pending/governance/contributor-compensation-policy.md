# Contributor Compensation Policy

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how the DAO compensates individuals who contribute work under its authority, including Working Group Stewards, Working Group contributors, and contracted specialists.

It establishes:

* Categories of compensated contributors
* How compensation is set and approved
* Payment types and mechanics
* Expense reimbursement rules
* Disclosure and transparency requirements

This policy does not govern ecosystem grants awarded to external projects — those are defined in the **Grant Program Policy**.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

* **Working Group Stewards** — elected leads accountable for WG outcomes
* **Working Group Contributors** — individuals engaged by a WG to deliver defined work
* **Contracted Specialists** — external individuals or entities engaged for specific deliverables

Token holders exercising governance rights are not compensated under this policy for ordinary participation.

All individuals and entities eligible for compensation under this policy must satisfy the KYC requirement defined in §3.5 before any payment is released.

---

## 3. Compensation Principles

### 3.1 Purpose-Alignment

Compensation must be in exchange for genuine services that advance the DAO's objectives. The DAO does not distribute funds to participants absent a clear service relationship.

---

### 3.2 Governance Authorization

All compensation must be authorized by DAO governance, either:

* Directly — via a governance-approved budget for a Working Group or role, or
* Indirectly — via WG discretion to allocate within a governance-approved budget

No compensation may be paid outside an approved budget.

---

### 3.3 Proportionality

Compensation should be proportionate to the nature, scope, and market rate of the work performed. Working Groups approving compensation are responsible for ensuring rates are reasonable.

---

### 3.4 Transparency

All compensation arrangements, rates, and payments must be publicly disclosed as part of regular Working Group reporting. No off-ledger compensation arrangements are permitted.

---

### 3.5 KYC Prerequisite

No compensation may be released to any individual or entity that has not completed KYC verification through the DAO's designated process. This requirement applies to:

* Working Group Stewards
* Working Group Contributors
* Contracted Specialists
* Any individual or entity receiving expense reimbursement

KYC must be completed before the first payment of an engagement. Existing engaged contributors who have not yet completed KYC must do so before their next payment cycle. The RAC maintains a record of KYC-verified payees. Working Group Stewards are responsible for confirming KYC status before authorising any payment from an approved budget.

KYC records are held confidentially and are not published publicly, except where disclosure is required by law.

---

## 4. Contributor Categories and Compensation Models

### 4.1 Working Group Stewards

Stewards are elected by DAO governance and serve fixed terms.

* Steward compensation rates are included in the Working Group's budget proposal submitted to governance
* Governance approval of the budget constitutes approval of the stated Steward rates
* Stewards may not set or approve their own compensation without separate governance authorization
* Compensation is paid on a regular schedule (recommended: monthly) for the duration of the term

---

### 4.2 Working Group Contributors

Contributors are engaged by Stewards within the WG's approved budget.

Compensation may be structured as:

* **Retainer** — fixed periodic payment for ongoing availability and contribution
* **Milestone-based** — payment upon delivery of defined deliverables (preferred for project work)
* **Hourly/daily rate** — payment for time-based engagements with defined scope and cap

The engagement type, rate, deliverables, and payment schedule must be documented and published in the WG's contributor registry before work begins.

---

### 4.3 Contracted Specialists

For specific expertise not available within a Working Group, Stewards may engage external contractors:

* Engagements above the threshold defined in DAO Parameters require a formal RFP process (see Section 6)
* Engagements below the threshold may be approved by Stewards within budget and disclosed in reporting
* All contractor engagements must be documented with a written scope of work

---

## 5. Payment Denominations

The DAO may pay contributors in:

* **XRD** — at a rate agreed at the time of engagement; market rate risk borne by the contributor
* **Stablecoins** — where available within the treasury and approved by governance
* **Mixed** — a combination of the above, as defined in the engagement terms

Payment denomination must be specified in the contributor's engagement terms before work begins. Currency preferences may be stated by contributors but are subject to treasury availability and governance approval.

---

## 6. RFP Process

For work above the threshold defined in DAO Parameters, Working Groups must use a Request for Proposal (RFP) process.

### 6.1 RFP Publication

The Working Group publishes an RFP on the governance forum containing:

* Description of the work required
* Deliverables and acceptance criteria
* Timeline
* Budget range
* Evaluation criteria
* Submission deadline (minimum **14-day** open period)

---

### 6.2 Bid Submission

Any eligible individual or entity may submit a proposal in response to an RFP.

Submissions must include:

* Proposed approach
* Qualifications and relevant experience
* Itemized cost breakdown
* Proposed timeline
* Conflict of interest disclosures (per **Conflict of Interest Policy**)

---

### 6.3 Evaluation and Selection

WG Stewards evaluate submissions against the published criteria. For engagements above a higher threshold (defined in DAO Parameters), an independent review panel may be required.

Selection must be:

* Documented with rationale
* Published on the governance forum
* Open to community comment for **7 days** before engagement is confirmed

---

### 6.4 Engagement Confirmation

Once selected, the engagement is confirmed by a written scope of work document signed (or on-chain acknowledged) by both parties. Payment is only released against this confirmed scope.

---

## 7. Milestone Disbursement

For milestone-based engagements:

* Payment is released upon Steward sign-off that a milestone has been met
* Deliverables must match the accepted scope of work
* Disputed milestone completions are escalated to the RAC for determination
* Partial payment for partial delivery may be agreed in the scope of work

---

## 8. Expense Reimbursement

Stewards and contributors may be reimbursed for reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred in service of the DAO:

* Expenses must be pre-authorized or within a defined per-person allowance (set in DAO Parameters)
* Claims must be submitted with receipts within **60 days** of the expense
* Claims are reviewed and approved by the relevant WG Stewards (or the RAC for Steward claims)
* Travel and event expenses above the threshold in DAO Parameters require advance approval

---

## 9. Vesting and Long-Term Incentives

For roles requiring sustained multi-term commitment, the DAO may approve token-based incentive arrangements with vesting schedules:

* Such arrangements require a governance-approved Treasury proposal
* Vesting schedules must be publicly disclosed
* Unvested tokens are forfeited upon voluntary departure or removal for misconduct
* Governance retains authority to modify vesting arrangements for cause via governance vote

---

## 10. Compensation Disclosure

All compensation arrangements are subject to full public disclosure:

* WG budget proposals must itemize Steward compensation
* Contributor registries must be published and maintained by each WG
* Quarterly WG reports must include a summary of compensation paid in the period
* The RAC may request disclosure of any engagement at any time

Contributors subject to the **Conflict of Interest Policy** must ensure their compensation from the DAO is included in their standing disclosure.

---

## 11. Prohibition on Unauthorized Payments

The following are prohibited:

* Paying individuals who have not been engaged under a documented, budget-authorized arrangement
* Retroactive compensation for work not previously agreed under this policy
* Compensation to a Steward's affiliated entities without disclosure and RAC approval
* Any arrangement designed to circumvent the transparency requirements of this policy
